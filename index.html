About

I am an economist working at the intersection of health economics and development economics. I received PhD in Economics from Visva-Bharati, a Central University and Institute of National Importance in India. My research examines maternal and child health and ageing-related vulnerabilities in developing economies. Broadly, I study how household decision-making structures, social networks, and institutional contexts shape health outcomes and health-seeking behaviour in low- and middle-income countries. Methodologically, I combine theoretical modelling with applied microeconometric analysis using large-scale survey datasets such as NFHS, IHDS, and LASI. I also have experience in designing and implementing primary surveys, including questionnaire development, field coordination, and data management.
